Service of process checklist

Improper service is the easiest win you'll ever get for a client. Here's what to check, before the clock starts.

0% through this course
§ 1 The scenario

Maria walks into your clinic on a Tuesday. Her landlord taped a notice to her door on Saturday night. She has been a tenant for six years and never missed rent. She is supposed to be in Housing Court next Friday — eight business days from now.

Before you read a single word of the notice, you are checking how it got there.

§ 2 The rule

A summary process summons is only valid if it was served by a constable or sheriff using one of three accepted methods. Taping it to a door by the landlord is not one of them.

The three valid methods
  1. In-hand delivery to the tenant by a sheriff or constable.
  2. Service to a competent adult at the tenant's residence — not a minor, not a guest.
  3. Last-and-usual + first-class mail by an authorized officer. Both must occur.
§ 3 What changed
Law changed · 3 days ago Ch. 167 · effective immediately

Defective service no longer just delays — defaults from before 2024 can now be sealed.

Until last week, you raised improper service to buy time. Now, the amendments to M.G.L. c. 239 §16 mean that if the original service was defective, the resulting default judgment is presumptively sealable on a single motion. See the next topic for the new motion language.

§ 4 Try it

One question. You'll see the answer once you choose.

Scenario

The notice was left under Maria's door by the landlord's nephew, who is 16. There is no constable's return of service. Maria found it Saturday evening. Is service valid?

The answer: a landlord cannot serve the summons themselves, and family members — especially minors — do not qualify as competent adults under the rules. You are going to ask the court to dismiss for defective service, and, if a default already exists, to seal it.
§ 5 If you remember nothing else
  • Who served it matters as much as what it says.
  • Only three methods qualify: in-hand, competent adult, or last-and-usual plus mail.
  • Defective service used to delay. Now it can erase the default.
